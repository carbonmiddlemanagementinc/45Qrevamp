A three-tier concentration-based credit structure: below 30% CO₂ (hard industrial), above 30% CO₂ (high-concentration), and direct air capture. The 30% threshold separates sectors where capture requires genuine post-combustion separation work from those producing near-pure CO₂ streams as a process byproduct.

Genuine post-combustion separation required. Chemical absorption with high energy penalty. The sectors with fewest decarbonization alternatives — cement, steel, SMR flue gas, coal power.

Above 30% CO₂ in flue stream
CO₂ already largely separated as a process necessity. Main cost is compression, dehydration, and transport/storage. Credit should cover T&S costs plus a reasonable margin — not generate large rents.
Ethanol fermentation (>85%), ammonia synthesis loop (~95–100%), natural gas processing (~95–100%), pre-PSA SMR syngas (15–25% at high pressure)

~420 ppm CO₂ (ambient atmosphere)
Categorically different from point-source capture. Processing ambient air requires massive volume throughput and high energy input. Credit functions partly as a learning-curve subsidy, not just cost coverage.
Solid sorbent DAC (Heirloom, Carbon Engineering), liquid solvent DAC (Climeworks), all atmospheric CO₂ removal
